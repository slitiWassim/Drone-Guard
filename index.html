Drone-Guard: A Self-Supervised Deep Learning Framework for Spatiotemporal Anomaly Detection in UAV Surveillance Videos

1SECROM Laboratory, EPT, University of Carthage, La Marsa 2078, Tunisia,
2ISITCOM, University of Sousse, Hammam Sousse 4011, Tunisia

Abstract

Anomaly detection is a critical component of video surveillance, enabling the timely identification of unusual or suspicious activities. Recent advancements in deep learning and computer vision have significantly improved the performance of anomaly detection systems. However, most existing methods face a trade-off between detection accuracy and computational efficiency. High-performing models are typically resource-intensive, rendering them impractical for deployment on edge devices with limited processing capabilities. Conversely, lightweight models often fail to maintain high accuracy, particularly in complex real-world scenarios. With the growing proliferation of Internet of Things (IoT) devices and the increasing adoption of drones in surveillance and defense applications, there is a pressing need for solutions that are both accurate and computationally efficient. To this end, we propose Drone-Guard, a novel framework for unsupervised anomaly detection in UAV video streams. At its core, our method employs a lightweight residual autoencoder enhanced with multi-stage feature extraction to effectively capture both local and global spatial patterns. To further enrich the representation, we introduce a Multi-Scale Grouped Query Attention (MS-GQA) mechanism that fuses multi-scale features, followed by Residual Vector Quantization (RVQ) to improve the compactness and quality of reconstructions. To overcome the scarcity of abnormal training data—a common challenge in unsupervised settings—we also propose a pseudo-anomaly synthesizer that generates synthetic anomalies using only normal data distributions. This enables robust model training without reliance on manually annotated anomalies. Extensive experiments and ablation studies conducted on benchmark datasets demonstrate that Drone-Guard achieves a superior trade-off between accuracy and efficiency, consistently outperforming state-of-the-art methods in constrained edge-computing environments. https://slitiwassim.github.io/Drone-Guard/.
